Why Furniture Assembly Takes Forever

You bought the bookshelf three weeks ago. It's still in the box. You opened it once, saw 47 pieces and a manual in Swedish, and closed it again.

Sound familiar?

Here's the thing. Those "simple" instructions assume you have four hours, the right tools, and the patience of a monk. Most people have none of those.

And when you finally start? You're halfway through and realize Step 12 was actually Step 11, but reversed. Now you have to take it all apart.

We see this every week. Half-built furniture, stripped screws, missing pieces that fell behind the couch. What should've been a Saturday project becomes a month-long eyesore.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to unbox everything before you arrive?
Nope. We handle that. Just have the boxes accessible. We'll unbox, assemble, and take the cardboard with us if you want.
What if parts are missing or damaged?
We check everything before we start. If something's wrong, we let you know immediately. You can decide whether to wait for replacement parts or proceed without that piece.
How long does assembly take?
Depends on the item. A simple desk takes 30-45 minutes. A full PAX wardrobe system takes 3-4 hours. We give you a time estimate when you call.
Do you assemble used furniture or items I'm moving?
Yes. Disassembly and reassembly for moves is something we do regularly. Just let us know.
Can you anchor furniture to the wall?
Absolutely. We recommend it for tall bookshelves and dressers, especially if you have kids. Safety first.
