Why DIY Electrical Work Is a Bad Idea

That light fixture looks simple to swap. Three wires, couple of screws. What could go wrong?

A lot, actually.

Electricity doesn't give warnings. You get it right, or you get hurt. And even if you don't get shocked, a bad connection today becomes a fire risk tomorrow.

We've seen it. Outlets wired backwards. Fixtures hanging by a thread with exposed copper. Junction boxes stuffed with too many wires and covered with painters tape. All done by someone who "watched a video."

Here's the thing. Most common electrical work isn't that hard. But it does require knowing what you're doing, having the right tools, and understanding why things are done a certain way. That's the difference between a working fixture and a fire hazard.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you a licensed electrician?
For major work (panel upgrades, new circuits, rewiring), we bring in our licensed master electrician. For common repairs and fixture swaps, NJ allows handyman work on existing circuits.
Why is my outlet dead?
Could be several things. Tripped breaker, tripped GFCI (even in another room), loose connection, or failed outlet. We diagnose before we fix.
Can you add a new outlet where there isn't one?
That usually requires running new wire and possibly a new circuit. Our licensed electrician handles that. Call us and we'll set it up.
What if my house has old wiring?
We work on older homes all the time. Some old wiring is fine and some should be replaced. We'll tell you honestly what we find.
Do I need to buy the fixture myself?
You can, or we can pick one up. If you buy it, make sure the electrical box can support the weight (especially for ceiling fans).
