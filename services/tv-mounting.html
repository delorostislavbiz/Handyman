Why TV Mounting Goes Wrong

That YouTube tutorial made it look easy. Fifteen minutes, three screws, done. Right?

Then you hit a stud in the wrong place. Or worse, you didn't hit a stud at all. Now there's a hole in your wall and your TV is still on the floor.

Here's what nobody mentions in those videos. A 65-inch TV weighs 50+ pounds. Mounted wrong, it's not just an eyesore. It's a safety hazard. TVs fall. They break. Sometimes they hurt people.

And those "universal" brackets? They're universal until they're not. Wrong bracket, wrong wall type, wrong height. You end up with a TV that tilts forward like it's judging you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does TV mounting take?
Most jobs take 1.5 to 2 hours. Brick or concrete walls take longer. We'll give you a time estimate when you call.
Can you mount a TV on brick or concrete?
Yes. We have masonry bits and the right anchors. It costs a bit more and takes a bit longer, but we do it regularly.
Do you hide the cables inside the wall?
For drywall, yes. We can run cables through the wall for a clean look. For brick or concrete, we use cable covers that match your wall color.
Do I need to buy the mount?
You can, but we recommend letting us handle it. We know which brackets work for which TVs. Wrong bracket means wobbly TV.
What if I already have a mount?
We'll use it if it's the right one. If it's not, we'll tell you before we start. No surprises.
