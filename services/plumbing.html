Why Small Plumbing Problems Become Big Ones

That toilet has been running for three months. It's just a little water, right?

Wrong. A running toilet wastes 200+ gallons per day. That's real money on your water bill. And that slow leak under the sink? It's feeding mold you can't see yet.

Here's the thing about plumbing. Small problems don't stay small. They get worse. That drip becomes a corroded pipe. That slow drain becomes a full clog. That "minor" leak becomes water damage that costs thousands to repair.

Most people wait because they think calling a plumber means $300 just to walk in the door. And for some companies, that's true. But common repairs don't require a master plumber with a truck full of equipment. They just need someone who knows what they're doing and has the right parts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you a licensed plumber?
For major plumbing work (pipe replacement, water heaters, main line), we bring in our licensed master plumber. For common repairs and fixture swaps, licensing isn't required in NJ and we have 20+ years of experience.
What's the difference between repair and replacement?
Repair means fixing what you have (new cartridge, new flapper). Replacement means putting in a new fixture entirely. We'll tell you honestly which one makes sense.
Do I need to buy the faucet/toilet myself?
You can, or we can pick it up for you. If you buy it, just make sure it's compatible with your existing plumbing. Call us first if you're unsure.
What if the problem is bigger than expected?
We tell you before we do extra work. If we open up a cabinet and find a bigger issue, you'll know immediately. No surprise charges.
Do you work on old houses with old plumbing?
Yes. Northern NJ has a lot of older homes with older pipes. We're careful with old plumbing and know when to advise calling in a licensed plumber for replacement.
