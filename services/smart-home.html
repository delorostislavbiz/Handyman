Why Smart Home Setup Frustrates Everyone

The box says "easy install." The app says "just follow the steps." Three hours later, you're resetting your WiFi for the fifth time and the doorbell still shows offline.

We get it. These devices are marketed as plug-and-play. But here's what the marketing doesn't mention.

Your WiFi might not reach the front door. Your wiring might not be compatible. The app might need firmware updates before anything works. And if you have an older home? Good luck getting a smart lock to fit that vintage deadbolt.

Most people buy two or three smart devices and only one of them works reliably. The others sit half-installed, blinking error codes nobody understands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to buy the device first?
Yes, but we can advise you on what to buy. Some devices work better with certain setups. Call us before you purchase if you're unsure.
What if my WiFi doesn't reach the device location?
We can install WiFi extenders or mesh nodes. This is a common issue, especially for outdoor cameras and garage door openers.
Will my smart thermostat work with my old heating system?
Usually yes, but not always. If you have an older boiler or radiator heat, some thermostats won't work. We check compatibility before installation.
Can you connect everything to one app?
Depends on the devices. If they're all from the same brand (like all Ring), yes. Mixed brands sometimes need a hub like SmartThings or Alexa routines. We set up whatever works best.
What about hardwired vs. battery devices?
We install both. Hardwired is better (no battery changes), but requires compatible wiring. We'll tell you which option works for your home.
