Why DIY Drywall Patches Always Show

You went to Home Depot. You got the patch kit. You followed the instructions.

Now there's a slightly bulgy circle on your wall that's somehow a different texture than everything around it. And when the light hits it? You can see exactly where the patch is.

Here's the thing about drywall repair. The actual patching is maybe 20% of the job. The rest is matching. Matching the texture. Matching the level. Matching the finish so it blends with the surrounding wall.

Those patch kits don't tell you about feathering compound in multiple layers. They don't mention that matching orange peel texture takes practice. And they definitely don't cover how different parts of your house might have different textures.

Badly patched drywall is worse than a hole. A hole you can explain. A bumpy, obvious patch just looks like you tried and failed.

How It Works

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does drywall repair take?
Depends on the size. A single small hole can be done same-day. Larger repairs need 24-48 hours for compound to dry between coats.
Will the patch be visible?
Not when we're done. The key is proper texture matching and blending. That's what we focus on.
Do you fix water-damaged drywall?
Yes, but the water source needs to be fixed first. If you still have a leak, we can refer a plumber. No point patching wet drywall.
Can you repair plaster walls?
Yes. Plaster requires different techniques than drywall. We use appropriate materials for plaster repair, not just drywall compound.
Do you paint after repair?
We can. We prime all patches (required before painting) and can paint the area to match if you want. Let us know when you call.
